CMA CGM Reflection - Checked

The company industry day presentation for CMA CGM was very informative and varied. The main presentation was split into four parts, each lasting 30 minutes with different speakers. The first segment focused on introducing the company and its history. They are a company that began as an international shipping company between France and the rest of the world and began expanding their reach, acquiring new companies, and researching/developing new technologies to improve their productivity. Seeing the progress the company has made in the past 30 years, along with what the company has acquired through its merging and alliances of several companies, was impressive. Something I thought was interesting was that the company is large enough to sponsor the local soccer team.

The next segment focused on efforts in decarbonisation, stating its several ventures into creating sustainable reusable energy. It has pioneered the use of biofuels for cargo ships, which has reduced the CO2 emissions per container ship in the EU by 50% over the past 15 years. Their main goal is to reach carbon neutrality by 2050. I found it interesting that they are going through such lengths to lower carbon emissions, and am glad to see the progress they are making. I hope that their efforts will reach companies in the US, so that they will follow in their footsteps.

The third segment focused on research and development, as well as internships within the company. This segment did not have slides to follow along with, but they did talk about their startup support that they host. In this program, they take on several different startup companies and projects and give them the resources they need to make their goals a reality. In most cases these companies and projects end in success, but there are some cases where they must be canceled due to lack of success. I like how they are willing to support so many startups that may have struggled getting their footing or funding otherwise, and I appreciate that their internship program seems fairly accommodating and educational, though it is unfortunate that the internships are only available at the Marseille corporate headquarters.

The final segment was more focused on the cloud computing and cloud standards used and emphasized by the company. They discussed how they have begun adopting cloud computing into their company as the concept of cloud computing grows, as well as where they have implemented its applications. They shared how their database of customers and resources have been digitized and stored in the cloud, where they can be more easily maintained and accessible. They have also created applications that allow customers to see exactly where their shipments are at all times. It was good to see them talk about their use of cloud computing and how they have upheld the standards imposed by NIST, IEEE, and other such established groups. The presentation seemed mostly geared towards the solid mechanics group, so it was good to see them appealing to the cloud computing students as well.
