Eagle Project: Horn Shields

I participated in Boy Scouts growing up, and achieved my Eagle rank, which is the highest rank you can achieve. In order to earn this rank (among earning the required merit badges), you must complete an Eagle project, in which you create a structure or service that in some way benefits the community. For my project, I built French Horn shields for my high school band. What these shields do is reflect the sound of the horn out to the audience, since the sound is typically projecting backwards. This project took a lot planning and management, from ordering the supplies to recruiting others to help build them to organizing a day for everyone to come together and build them. In the end, the shields were built and delivered to the band, where they are still being used to this day.

Mission Trip: Hurricane Harvey

As part of my church (St. Philips), we regularly organize mission trips to areas that are in need of help, such as areas that were recently hit by natural disasters. For several of our trips, we traveled to Rockport, TX (located on the coast of the Gulf of Mexico) to assist with the cleanup efforts from Hurricane Harvey. The year that it hit, we went down and built a ramp for a couple who were unable to walk but lived in an elevated house, so when the hurricane hit, it destroyed their path of getting out of the house. We were able to get the ramp built in a week, and they were finally able to leave their house for the first time since the storm. The next year, we went back to continue the cleanup as a result of there still being a lot of damaged structures and debris. This time, we helped organize and renovate a charity location for supplying food and clothes to those who had been displaced during the storm and could not find other living situations. We were able to successfully organize the contents of the house so the process of assisting others could be streamlined.

Feed My Starving Children

I have participated in several events hosted by Feed My Starving Children, where we package food away for those who are in need of it. We will gather in a large room and form assembly lines, with some handling the food assembly, some packaging the food away, and some taking the food boxes away to be shipped to other countries. Often times, I was on packaging duty since the food handling was meant for more experienced workers who knew how to make it without contaminating it, and the carrying of the boxes was often done by lifters and older men. Though I felt like I had a small role in the whole process, I know that what was being done could not have been done by just a few people, and every part of the process mattered, as the more people we had, the more food we were able prepare and ship out.
